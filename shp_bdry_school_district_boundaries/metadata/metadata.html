Section 1: Overview

Originator:Minnesota Department of Education

Title: School District Boundaries, Minnesota, SY2019-2020

Abstract: This file represents the boundaries of all independent (public), common, and special school districts in the state of Minnesota. Minnesota school districts report any changes to their elementary, middle and high school attendance area boundaries annually. This school district file was dissolved from the statewide attendance boundary file to show just the statewide school district boundaries. Since 2008, district boundaries have been modified based on county parcels. (Parcel data are supplied by counties to MnGeo, who in turn share that with MDE.)

PLEASE NOTE: For the 2019-2020 school year, the web mapping service for this layer has moved.

Purpose: This school district boundary information is developed as a GIS data set and, along with additional information, is used by the Minnesota Department of Education to identify program financial priorities.

Section 2: Data Quality

Attribute Accuracy: Each school district checks the previous year's map for accuracy of school names and boundaries. Changes are verified by the Minnesota Department of Education. School district boundaries are dissolved from a larger statewide school district file containing elementary, middle and high school attendance boundaries. Boundaries are maintained within an enterprise geodatabase.

Logical Consistency: Data are topologically structured and verified.

Completeness: All independent, common, and special school districts within Minnesota are included. Prior publications included a boundary for the Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport; in order to facilitate relationships to the MDE ORG database, this record was removed and dissolved into the Richfield Area school district boundary.

Horizontal Positional Accuracy: Positional accuracy of the data sources varies widely. Source scales range between 1:24,000 (or less) and 1:100,000. Many of the boundaries are hand-drawn by the school districts.

In an on-going process since 2008, school district boundaries have been modified to a greater accuracy using county parcel datasets. See notes on potential boundary inaccuracies under 'Lineage', particularly within metadata on historical versions of this dataset.


Lineage: For a detailed lineage of the development of this dataset, refer to the metadata packaged with the historical versions available on the MN Geospatial Commons.

NOTES ON MISMATCHES OF DATA:

1. The County Auditor's Office is required to delineate school district boundaries by law, and maintains the official record for tax purposes. Note that school districts also have boundary maps, and that, while the two sets of map boundaries should match, they do not always. In cases where the school district and the county auditor records show different boundaries, this should be resolved locally first before suggesting updates to MDE. It becomes an issue for the maintainers of statewide files when updates are obtained from both sources.

2. Mismatches between the Census Boundary lines and the School District lines: although the school district boundary file generally uses Census geography where possible, it does not always follow precisely. Therefore, mismatches between this file and school district boundaries maintained by the Census can be legitimate. As of 2019, MDE is updating Census versions via the Census School District Reporting Program (SDRP). However, due to the limitations of this program, there will always be mismatches, particularly along detailed borders such as those defined by water bodies.

For 2020, the SDORGID column was added for transparency on unique identifier linkage to the MDE ORG database. ORGID was also added for consistency with related datasets.

Detailed overview of columns:
SDORGID - unique numeric identifier used to link with the MDE ORG database.

ORGID - This is a concatenated character field containing UNI_TYP, UNI_MAJ and 6 following 0's, which can be used to identify school districts.
Example: Aitkin is 010001000000 (01 0001 000 000).
Example: Brandon-Evansville is 012908000000 (01 2908 000 000).

UNI_TYP - This is an MDE two character identifier describing the type of school district
"01" - Independent Districts and Schools
"02" - Common Districts and Schools: Franconia-0323, Prinsburg-0815
"03" - Special Districts and Schools: Minneapolis-0001, South St. Paul-0006

UNI_MAJ - Four-character school district number, left-padded with zeroes.
0001 (Aitkin) to 2908 (Brandon-Evansville)

UNI_NAM - "preferred name" of the School District from the MDE ORG database. Prior publications of this data used a simplified name.

WEB_URL - School district website, where available, from the MDE ORG database.

SQMILES - Square miles for each district, derived from the shape of the polygon.

ACRES - Acres for each district, derived from the shape of the polygon.

Detailed Citation: More current information on school districts can be obtained from MDE ORG at https://public.education.mn.gov/MdeOrgView/
